## Overview

Writing tasks progress from basic spelling to composing structured emails and opinion texts. You type on a **tablet/touch keyboard** — practice this!

---

## Task Type 1: Fill Missing Letters (pre-A1)

**Format**: A word is shown with gaps → type the missing letters.

**Example**:
> _ch_le → **Schule**
> Kra_kenh_us → **Krankenhaus**

**Strategy**:
- Focus on common German spelling patterns: sch, ch, ei, ie, au, äu, ß/ss
- Practice the vocabulary from all 10 SDS domains
- Watch for umlauts: ä, ö, ü — know where they appear in common words

---

## Task Type 2: Copy Details into Form Fields (pre-A1 / A1)

**Format**: Given a text (letter, card, message), fill in a form with specific details (name, address, phone, date, etc.).

**Example**:
> Text: *"Ich bin Maria Schneider, wohne in Bern, Hauptstrasse 12, Tel. 031 234 56 78."*
>
> Form:
> - Name: **Maria Schneider**
> - Stadt: **Bern**
> - Adresse: **Hauptstrasse 12**
> - Telefon: **031 234 56 78**

**Strategy**:
- Read the form fields first — know what info you need
- Copy exactly (correct spelling, numbers, format)
- Don't confuse similar fields (PLZ vs. Telefon, Vorname vs. Nachname)

---

## Task Type 3: Reply to Text Messages (A1)

**Format**: You see a text message → write a short reply (2–4 sentences).

**Example**:
> Message: *"Hallo! Kommst du morgen zur Party? Ab 19 Uhr bei Lisa."*
>
> Your reply: *"Hallo! Ja, ich komme gerne. Soll ich etwas mitbringen? Bis morgen!"*

**Strategy**:
- Keep it simple and natural
- Answer the question(s) in the message
- Add one follow-up question or comment
- Use informal register (du, no formal greeting needed)

---

## Task Type 4: Write a Simple Email (A2)

**Format**: Given a situation → write an email (5–8 sentences).

**Common prompts**:
- Cancel or reschedule an appointment
- Ask a question about a product or service
- Respond to an invitation

**Example prompt**:
> You have a doctor's appointment on Thursday at 10:00. You can't come. Write an email: apologize, explain why, ask for a new appointment.

**Example answer**:
> *Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,*
>
> *ich habe am Donnerstag um 10 Uhr einen Termin bei Ihnen. Leider kann ich nicht kommen, weil ich arbeiten muss. Könnten Sie mir bitte einen neuen Termin geben? Am Freitag oder nächste Woche wäre gut für mich.*
>
> *Vielen Dank und freundliche Grüsse*
> *[Name]*

**Strategy**:
- Always include: greeting → reason → request → closing
- See `05-grammar/email-phrases.md` for ready-made phrases
- Keep sentences short and clear

---

## Task Type 5: Request Information (B1)

**Format**: Write an email requesting information about a course, service, or event.

**Example prompt**:
> You want to take a German course. Write to the language school: ask about prices, times, levels, and start dates.

**Structure**:
1. Greeting (formal)
2. Say why you're writing
3. Ask 3–4 specific questions
4. Thank them
5. Formal closing

---

## Task Type 6: Opinion / Pro-Contra Text (B2)

**Format**: Given a topic → write ~200 words with pros, cons, and your opinion.

**Example prompt**:
> *"Sollten Geschäfte am Sonntag geöffnet sein? Schreiben Sie Ihre Meinung mit Argumenten dafür und dagegen."*

**Structure** (use this template):
1. Introduction — state the topic
2. Arguments FOR (2 points)
3. Arguments AGAINST (2 points)
4. Your position + conclusion

**Example connectors to use**:
- Einerseits… andererseits… (On one hand… on the other hand…)
- Ein Vorteil ist, dass… / Ein Nachteil ist, dass…
- Meiner Meinung nach… / Ich bin der Meinung, dass…
- Zusammenfassend kann man sagen, dass…

- Many tasks had a **work theme** — prepare work-related emails especially well

## General Writing Tips

1. **Practice on a tablet keyboard** — this is the #1 bottleneck reported by test-takers
2. **Memorize email structures** — greeting, body, closing for formal + informal
3. **Use connectors** — even simple ones (und, aber, weil, deshalb) improve your score
4. **Watch your spelling** — umlauts, capitalization of nouns, ß/ss
5. **Answer ALL parts of the prompt** — if it says "apologize + explain + suggest new date", do all three
